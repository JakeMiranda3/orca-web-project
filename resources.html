Research On Population

This study highlights that this endangered orca population, already struggling due to the decline in Chinook salmon (their primary prey), is now facing accelerated decline from climate change, pollution, and vessel disturbances. They used long-term as well as recent data, which showed the survival and reproductive rates of Orcas have dropped with fewer calves surviving to adulthood. This article warns viewers that if this continues, this population will face an increased risk of extinction. Warning sign of an accelerating decline in critically endangered killer whales (Nature Communications).
